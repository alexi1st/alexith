Top 10 Puzzle Cabin Moments

MY NAME IS ALEXIS and I am an avid puzzle lover. I may not have the best credentials or the longest experience, but trust me, I know ball.

For quite some time, I was doing at least one 300 pc puzzle every single day for like, two, almost three, months straight. Not to mention, I was doing like, on a GOOD DAY, upmost to 5 puzzles. Babs I was INNITTT. Okay, mate? And not to mention, I've done 2 1000pc puzzles this summer.

So if you think about it, I did 173 300 pc puzzles. 173 x 300 is 51,900. Plus the additional 2000 pieces from the physical puzzles, I handled an estimated 53,900 pieces of puzzles this summer. Some physical, most digital. My average time on a NIGHTMARE puzzle in puzzle cabin is 53 minutes. However, it wasn't always that way.

Throughout the summer, my daily puzzles were not only a testament of my devotion to puzzling, but also a journey of pushing myself harder and harder to decrease my average puzzle time. I remember working my way down from a pathetic 1 hr 16 min. It was a lot of stress, pressure, and frustration--but amidst it all, it was truly resilience, patience, and focus that got me through it.

Mane what am I even saying. Basically, I played and did A LOT of puzzles on puzzle cabin last summer. So I'm qualified to give my say on what the top puzzles I did this summer were. OK? I share now

TOP TEN PUZZLE CABIN PUZZLES I'VE DONE...

  1. Macarons


    A macarons puzzle needed a place on here. Every single macaron one was fun, easy,
    stress-free, and fulfilling. So shoutout to the macarons, you made it on here, but
    they barely made it because aesthetically, they are not as pleasing.

  2. Matcha Tea

    I had to put this one on here because I remember when I first found this photo,
    I was very excited to do it as a puzzle. I do remember feeling very satisfied. Even
    though I hate doing greenery, this one wasn't memorably difficult with its leaves.

  3. Fun Breakfast Spread

    This one feels a bit like an outlier on this list. In terms of aesthetics, this
    one is not necessarily my ideal photo for a puzzle. However, I just remember
    this one in particular being quite enjoyable to do because of the different stuff.

  4. Apple Cinnamon

    This one was really memorable for me. It was good vibes doing this one. It was
    also stress free and simple, plus the star-cut apple slices are really doing it
    for me. This was one of the first puzzles I got a lower time of ~39 minutes.

  5. Gurt: Yo

    With the amount of yogurt/smoodie/acai bowl images I downloaded, they needed to make
    this list. Not only is there 4 bowls (variety), but the toppings are aesthetically
    pleasing as well. The cloth was annoying to do but the bowls made it worth it.

  6. Artistic Gurt

    Another bowl makes it onto this list. This one in particular earns its place
    because this yogurt bowl (?) presentation is just stellar and unforgettable. I
    really like the artistic tray, and the variety and aesthetic of the toppings are unmatched.

  7. Diverse Donut Display

    If there's one thing I fw in a puzzle, it's VARIETY. I like photos that
    are centered around one specific thing (in this one it's donuts), but it shows
    us a diverse range of said thing. I like it. This made me realize why DEI matters.

  8. Duck Face Bread

    I remember this photo so fondly because this photo with its duck-face bread
    really made me excited to do this puzzle. The aesthetics of this one are simple--
    neutrals but with that vibrancy of the green slice and the green background. It's a
    perfect mix with these chunky breads.

  9. Circular Matcha Sweets Display

    I wont' lie, I was skeptical of putting this one on here--let alone this high
    on the list. My justification on this one is it was very fun finding the pieces
    that make up the circular display. They're the easiest parts to differentiate, and it
    was rewarding to piece them to together. Not to mention, building the display allowed it
    to be sort of a "bridge" between all the other pieces so they'd all connect,,, if you know
    what I mean. But yeah, the little treats also provide me with that much loved variety I
    talk so much about!

  10. Purple Sweet Stimulation Station

    THIS PUZZLE. TAKES. THE CAKE. Okay, maybe a controversial pick (honestly I
    think my entire list is), but let me justify.

    When it comes to puzzles of this scale (big scene, small details), it's very
    easy to get overwhelmed and frustrated when you can't finish it before your
    "average time". However, this one in particular was just so fun to do. Although
    everything is purple and at the start it all starts to just blend together,
    once you commit yourself to it, you'll find that these small detailed pieces are
    must easier to understand than you thought. It's actually quite easy and
    enjoyable to piece the detailed pieces together compared to if you were piecing parts
    that make up a bigger subject of a puzzle, since all pieces will be less clarifying
    and essentially look the same.

    Anyways, THAT is my reasoning behind this puzzle. I enjoyed this one a lot,
    and this made me realize the art in these "smaller detail, but bigger picture" puzzles.

Conclusion

Welp, that pretty much concludes my list for my Top 10 Puzzle Cabin Puzzles. Maybe you don't agree
with some of these or maybe you do. Maybe you can't understand why I would make this list. Quite frankly, I don't know why
I made this either. But if I had a nickel for everytime I made an "blog" for Puzzle Cabin, I'd have two nickels. Which
might be a world record because I haven't seen any Puzzle Cabin blog that wasn't by me.

Honorable Mention

A Flags of the World puzzle is my ideal puzzle. I've probably done this
one like at least 5 times. It usually takes over an hour to finish, but the main reason I keep
going back to it is to figure out the best strats to finish it quicker. So yeah, honorable
mention right here.
